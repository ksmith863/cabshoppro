05 — Quotes & Invoices

Quotes & Invoices

Build professional quotes with line items, markup, and tax. Send for client approval with one click. Convert approved quotes to invoices and collect payment online.

Quotes & Invoices

Creating a Quote

1
Click + New QuoteOpens the quote editor. Link it to a project and client.
2
Add line itemsEach line sources three ways: pull from 📦 Inventory (decrements stock automatically), pull from 📚 Item Library, or type a custom one-off entry.
3
Set markup and taxApply markup per line or a global tax rate. Margins and sell prices calculate automatically.
4
Add supporting documentsAttach T&Cs, renderings, or spec sheets in the Supporting Documents section.
5
Run a Profitability CheckClick 📊 Profitability Check to review total cost, gross profit, and margin — with every line ranked lowest-margin-first — before anything goes to the client.
6
Send for approvalClick ✉ Send Quote — client receives a branded email with the full quote and an approval link.

Hiding Line Items From the Client

Every line item has a 🔒 Hide from Client checkbox. A hidden line's cost still counts fully toward the quote total, but won't appear individually in the email, PDF, or client approval page — useful for granular components like fasteners or small consumables you want to track without itemizing for the client. Group several hidden items under a section set to Client View: Summary to show one rolled-up line instead of a long itemized list.

Client Approval Flow

When a client receives a quote email, they click the link to open a secure approval page. They can review line items, sign digitally, and approve — you get notified instantly and the quote status updates to Approved.

Client Approval Page

Converting to Invoice

Once approved, open the quote and click Convert to Invoice. The invoice inherits all line items. Set a due date, add payment terms, and send.

Online Payment

On any invoice, click 💳 Payment Link to generate a Stripe-powered payment page for the exact invoice amount. The client can pay by card immediately. The link is included automatically when you email the invoice.

Quote & Invoice Statuses

StatusMeaning
DraftNot yet sent to client
SentEmailed to client, awaiting approval
ApprovedClient has signed and approved
DeclinedClient declined
UnpaidInvoice sent, payment pending
PaidPayment received
Tip: The quote editor remembers where you were — if you refresh the page while editing a quote, it reopens to the same quote automatically.

06 — Finish Estimator

Finish Estimator

A material take-off and cost calculator for cabinet finishing, under Quotes & Invoices. Works out gallons needed, container counts, labor, and consumables — then hands a single clean line item to a quote.

Finish Estimator

How It Works

1
Set job quantitiesCabinet count, finished faces per cabinet, average sq ft per face, and a waste/overlap factor.
2
Build finishing scenariosNamed, switchable scenarios (Painted, Stained, Sealer only by default) each with an ordered list of finishing steps.
3
Set each step's coveragePick a Finish Category to auto-fill a typical published coverage range, target DFT, and transfer efficiency by equipment type — or enter your own product's real spec.
4
Set labor and consumablesLabor rate, hours per cabinet, and consumables as a % of materials, flat $/cabinet, or flat $ for the job.
5
Add to QuoteSend the calculated total — material, labor, and consumables combined — as one line item, with the full breakdown saved in the description.
Note: Material usage uses the formula real shops rely on — (surface area × coats × target DFT) ÷ coverage @ 1 mil ÷ transfer efficiency — not a generic spread-rate guess. Coverage defaults are typical published ranges by finish category, not a specific product's verified spec; swap in your own product's TDS number for an accurate estimate.

07 — Cabinet Estimator

Cabinet Estimator

A rate-card based calculator under Quotes & Invoices for pricing cabinetry by type, style, and grade — per cabinet or per linear foot.

Cabinet Estimator

Setting Up Your Rate Card

1
Click ⚙ Rate CardSet your shop's standard rate per cabinet type — Base, Wall, Tall, Specialty — including default width, $/cabinet, and $/linear foot.
2
Set style and grade adjustmentsCabinet Style (Frameless/Euro Overlay, Face Frame Inset, Beaded Face Frame Inset, Other) and Finish Grade (Paint, Stain) each apply as a % adjustment stacked on the base rate.

Pricing a Job

1
Pick a project (optional)Link to an existing project, or name a new one — picking an existing project links the resulting quote to it automatically.
2
Enter quantities per typeQuantity, width per unit, and per-cabinet or per-linear-foot pricing — mix and match by type.
3
Tag style and gradeInternal only — never shown on the client-facing quote, but saved on the line for future cost-history reporting.
4
Add to QuoteSend one combined line, or one line per cabinet type, to a new or existing quote.
Tip: The Cabinet Analytics Tag (Type, Style, Grade) can also be set directly on any quote line item in the Quote Builder — useful for tagging lines that weren't created through this calculator.

10 — Finance

Finance

Track income and expenses, manage your chart of accounts, and export to QuickBooks-compatible CSV.

Transactions

Log any income or expense transaction and assign it to a Chart of Accounts category. Filter by date range, type, or category to see exactly where your money is going. For purchases with a physical receipt, use the dedicated Receipts page instead — it captures the photo, vendor, and cost breakdown, and creates the transaction automatically.

Chart of Accounts

Customize your account categories in Admin Settings → 📋 Chart of Accounts. Default categories include Revenue, Material Costs, Labor, and Operating Expenses.

QuickBooks Export

Go to Admin Settings → 📊 QuickBooks to export:

  • Chart of Accounts (QBO-formatted CSV)
  • Transactions
  • Invoices

Import these files into QuickBooks Online via the import tool. Full OAuth live sync is planned for a future update.

Profitability Dashboard

Navigate to Profitability in the sidebar for a full breakdown by project — revenue, material costs, labor hours and cost, gross profit, and margin percentage. Compare projects side by side and identify your most and least profitable work.

12 — Inventory

Inventory

Track stock levels for materials, hardware, and supplies. Get alerted when anything runs low. Inventory and the Material & Item Library share the same category list, so stock data stays unified across both.

Adding Items to Inventory

The simplest path: in the Item Library's New Item form, flip on 📦 Track this item in Inventory to reveal Qty on Hand, Reorder Threshold, Status, Supplier, and a Low-Stock Alert Note — one form creates and keeps both the catalog item and its inventory record in sync. Items can also be added directly from the Inventory page, or pushed there from an existing Item Library entry via the + Inventory button on its card.

Low Stock Alerts

Set a minimum quantity for any inventory item. When stock falls below that threshold, a notification appears in the dashboard and notification bell.

Usage Logging

Log material usage directly to a project to track what's been consumed and keep inventory counts accurate. Pulling an inventory item into a quote as a line item automatically decrements stock when the quote is saved.

Importing Supplier Price Lists

Click ⋯ Actions → Import Items or Price List and upload any CSV file. CabShop Pro auto-detects column names from common supplier formats — columns like Item, Description, Part#, Price/Cost, UOM, and Category are mapped automatically.

After import, items are categorized as "Supplier" and a reminder appears to add your markup before quoting.

Note: Inventory tracking is now opt-in per item via the toggle in the item form — turn it on for anything you want to count stock against, regardless of category.
