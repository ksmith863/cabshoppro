⬡ Getting Started

Signing in

Go to cabshoppro.com and enter your email and password. Use Forgot Password? to receive a reset link by email.

Navigating the app

The sidebar on the left is your main navigation, organized into sections:

  • WORKSPACE — Home dashboard, Projects, Tasks, Calendar
  • CLIENTS & SALES — CRM, Quotes & Invoices, Item Library
  • SHOP — Finance, Inventory, Tools, Purchase Orders, Profitability
  • LIBRARY — Documents, Media, Gallery, Samples
  • ACCOUNT — Admin Settings, Subscription, Help, Super Admin

◈ Projects

Projects are the core of CabShop Pro. Each project tracks financials, production stages, tasks, documents, quotes, notes, and change orders in one place.

Creating a project

  1. Click Projects in the sidebar, then + New Project.
  2. Optionally select a template at the top — this pre-fills the name, stages and default tasks (see Templates section below).
  3. Fill in project name, client, budget, dates and description.
  4. Click Save Project.

Saving a project as a template NEW

Once you have a project set up exactly how you like it — with the right stages and tasks — you can save it as a reusable template.

  1. Open any project card.
  2. Scroll to the bottom of the detail panel.
  3. Click ⭐ Save as Template in the footer.
  4. The template is saved and immediately available in the New Project modal.

Production Stages

Click the Stages tab inside any project to see its production pipeline. Each stage shows time logged, a progress indicator, and a photo attachment option.

  • Click the checkbox on a stage to mark it complete.
  • Click + Time to log hours worked on a stage.
  • Click the ▼ to expand the time log for a stage.
  • Click 📷 to attach a job-site photo (Pro plan).

Project Financials Tab

The Financials tab shows a complete breakdown of income, material costs, labour costs, and other expenses — with gross profit and margin at a glance.

Change Orders

Use the Changes tab to track scope changes, additional work requests, and their approval status.

📐 Project Templates NEW

Templates let you capture a project's structure — stages, default tasks, colour and description — so you can reuse it on future jobs of the same type.

Creating a template from a project

  1. Open any project you want to use as a template.
  2. Click ⭐ Save as Template in the project footer.
  3. The template is saved immediately with the project's name, colour, stages and all its tasks.

Editing a template

  1. Go to Admin Settings → 📐 Templates.
  2. Click on any template card to open the editor.
  3. Edit the name, description, icon, colour, stages and default tasks.
  4. Click Save Changes.

Using a template on a new project

When creating a new project, your saved templates appear as buttons at the top of the New Project form. Click one to pre-fill the name, stages, colour and description. Tasks from the template are automatically created when you save the project.

💡 You can still change the project name after selecting a template — the stages and tasks will still be applied correctly.

◎ CRM — Clients & Contacts

Track all your clients, partners, and suppliers in one place. Click any contact card to open a full detail view with their linked projects, items supplied, and communication history.

Contact types

  • 👤 Client — homeowners, builders, designers
  • 🤝 Partner — trades, subcontractors, installers
  • 🏭 Supplier — lumber yards, hardware vendors, finishers

Adding a contact

  1. Click + Contact in the top right of the CRM page.
  2. Select the contact type, fill in name, company, email, phone.
  3. Click Save Contact.
💡 Click anywhere on a contact card to open the full detail view — projects, history, and all linked information.

▦ Tasks

Tasks can be standalone (shop to-dos) or linked to a specific project. View them in a Kanban board or flat list.

Opening a task UPDATED

Click anywhere on a task card to open it for editing. Use the individual buttons (✓ checkbox, ＋ subtask, ✎ edit, × delete) for specific actions — they won't accidentally open the editor.

Creating a task

  1. Click + Task in the top right of the Tasks page.
  2. Enter a title, link to a project, set priority and due date.
  3. Click Save Task.
💡 You can also add tasks directly from inside a project — use the quick-add bar at the top of any open project card.

📅 Calendar

Schedule meetings, site visits, deadlines and deliveries. Events can be linked to projects and contacts.

Event types

  • 📋 Meeting · 🏠 Site Visit · 📦 Delivery · ⚠ Deadline · 🔧 Install · 📅 Other

Creating an event

  1. Click + Event or click a date on the calendar grid.
  2. Set the event type, title, date, time and link to a project or contact.
  3. Click Save Event.
💡 From a task with a due date, click 📅 to send it straight to the calendar as a Deadline event.

◑ Quotes & Invoices

Creating a quote

  1. Go to Quotes & Invoices, click + New Quote.
  2. Select a client, set an expiry date, and add line items.
  3. Each line item has: description, quantity, unit, unit cost, tax rate, and optional discount.
  4. Click Save Quote.

Sending for client approval

  1. Open a quote in Draft status.
  2. Click Send for Approval — this generates a secure approval link.
  3. Copy or email the link to your client. They click Approve or Decline — no login needed.
  4. You are notified in-app when they respond.

Converting to an invoice

Once a quote is approved, open it and click Convert to Invoice. The invoice tracks payment status (Unpaid → Partial → Paid).

Quote statuses

  • Draft — still being prepared
  • Sent — approval link shared with client
  • Approved / Declined — client has responded
  • Expired — past expiry date without a response

$ Finance Tracker

Log income and expenses, track job profitability, and export to QuickBooks.

Adding a transaction

  1. Click + Transaction.
  2. Choose type (Income or Expense), enter amount, date, description.
  3. Link to a project and assign a Chart of Accounts code.
  4. Check Business Overhead for non-job expenses (rent, utilities, etc.).

QuickBooks export

Go to Admin Settings → QuickBooks to export your Chart of Accounts, transactions, and invoices as CSV files formatted for QuickBooks import.

📦 Purchase Orders NEW

Track material and vendor orders with a full Purchase Order workflow. Find it under Shop → Purchase Orders in the sidebar.

Creating a Purchase Order

  1. Click + New PO.
  2. Enter the vendor name, link to a project (optional), set an expected delivery date.
  3. Add line items — each has a description, quantity, and unit cost.
  4. Click Create PO. The PO is saved as Draft.

PO status workflow

  • Draft → click Mark Sent after sending to vendor
  • Sent → click Mark Received when materials arrive
  • Received — complete
  • Partial / Cancelled — available via the Edit screen
💡 Link a PO to a project to keep all material ordering visible from the project view.

📈 Profitability Dashboard UPDATED

Get a financial overview of all active projects — revenue, expenses, profit, and margin — in one table. Find it under Shop → Profitability.

Sorting the table NEW

Click any column header to sort the table by that column. Click again to reverse the sort direction. An arrow (▲▼) shows the active sort.

Performance highlights panel

The top-right panel shows your Most Profitable and Needs Attention projects at a glance, plus a bar chart of top clients by revenue.

⊞ Inventory

Track sheet goods, lumber, hardware, finishing products, and supplies. Get low-stock alerts and log usage directly to projects.

Adding an inventory item

  1. Go to Inventory, click + Item.
  2. Enter name, category, unit, quantity, reorder point and cost.
  3. Click Save Item.

Logging usage

Open an item and click Log Usage. Enter the quantity used and link it to a project. The inventory count is automatically reduced.

Low-stock alerts

Items below their reorder point are highlighted in red with a ⚠ alert. Use Restock to update the count when new stock arrives.

🔧 Tools & Equipment

Maintain a register of all shop equipment — purchase details, warranty dates, depreciation, insurance value, and service history.

Adding a tool or machine

  1. Go to Shop → Tools & Equip, click + Tool.
  2. Fill in the tool name, category, manufacturer, serial number, purchase date and cost.
  3. Optionally add warranty expiry, asset tag, and location.
  4. Click Save Tool.
💡 Use the asset tag field to match your insurance inventory list. Warranty expiry dates are highlighted when they're coming up soon.

◫ Library

Documents (Resource Library)

Store and search SOPs, standards, templates, client specs, and terms & conditions. Link documents to specific projects for easy access.

Media Library

Upload photos, blueprints, and reference images. Tag by project, client, or product category.

Gallery

A portfolio-style view of your finished work. Organize by project or product category. Great for sharing with prospective clients.

Samples Library

Catalogue your physical door, finish, and hardware samples with photos, colours, and supplier info. Useful for showroom or client meetings.

◈ AI Features

CabShop Assistant (AI Chatbot)

The 💬 chat button in the bottom-right corner opens the CabShop AI Assistant. Ask it anything about using the app — feature how-tos, workflow questions, or shop-management tips.

AI Project Analysis

Inside any project, click the AI Analysis button to get an intelligent summary of that project's financial performance, time efficiency, and suggested improvements.

📝 AI features require a Pro or Team subscription.

⚙ Admin Settings

Manage company information, regional settings, users, templates, chart of accounts, and QuickBooks integration.

Tabs in Admin Settings

  • 🏢 Company — name, address, logo, contact details
  • 🌐 Regional — currency, date format, tax rates
  • 💳 Subscription — current plan, renewal date, alerts
  • 👥 Users — add and manage team members (Team plan)
  • 🔔 Notifications — alert thresholds and preferences
  • 📐 Templates — view, edit and delete project templates NEW
  • 📋 Chart of Accounts — add/edit/delete accounts for transaction coding
  • 📊 QuickBooks — export CSV files for import into QuickBooks

Managing templates NEW

Click any template card in the Templates tab to open the full editor. From there you can rename the template, change the icon and colour, add or remove production stages, and add, edit or delete default tasks. Click Save Changes when done.

⚠ Deleting a template only removes it from the template list — it does not affect any projects that were already created from it.
